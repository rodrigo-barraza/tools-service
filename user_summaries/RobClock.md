# RobClock — Profile Summary

> Based on forum posts, 10 sampled threads, and a historic Newgrounds presence.

RobClock was a deeply foundational member of the Clock Crew, joining the group in its very early days (post count ~9,419). As an animator, critic, and long-time forum regular, RobClock functioned as one of the community's stabilizing voices, often cutting through hype or drama with grounded, blunt assessments.

## Identity
- **Alias**: RobClock
- **Presence**: Often mentioned interchangeably with other founding or key early figures (like Blob). He was seen as a standard-bearer for traditional "Clock Quality." FloundermanClock famously noted: *"We will break him, and we will rebuild him... God knows blob and rob weren't great when they joined."*

## Artistic Talent & Output
- **Early Collaborator**: RobClock was instrumental in the early flash output that defined the crew. He was the author behind major community keystones like "StrawberryClock's B-Day" and "StrawberryClock's B-Day2", which were pivotal in establishing the shared universe of Clock Day.
- **Reviewer**: On Newgrounds, RobClock accrued massive engagement statistics (1,061 blams, 4,778 saves), indicating he spent thousands of hours actively moderating the Newgrounds portal and curating content.

## Personality & Humor
RobClock is fundamentally a "straight shooter." He rarely indulged in the over-the-top, absurdist random humor that defined newer members. 
- **The Pragmatist**: When younger members got excited about releasing massive meme dumps, RobClock brought them back down to earth: 
  > *"The joke is funny. But if anyone thinks these stupid 5 second collabs are bringing the cc over 2.5 on clockday, try again."*
- **Sarcastic Critic**: Often gave very curt, dry feedback on Flash submissions: *"Slightly over-animated, though."* or *"Silly man! Only one topic in the spam forum at a time!"*

## Community Role
- **The Quality Gatekeeper**: RobClock spent significant energy protecting the "Clock" brand from deteriorating into mindless spam. He was visibly frustrated when the original satirical edge of the Clock Crew developed into genuine low-effort posting.
- **Vocal Opponent of CC Decline**: He started threads explicitly calling out the laziness of newer Clocks ("Let's change our name to the Sprite Crew", "Can we stop trying to please newgrounds?").

## Interests & Tastes
- **Classic Flash Animation**: Advocated for actual drawing and traditional flash mechanics over tweening or sprite rips. 
- **Pop Culture Critiques**: Sampled threads include reviews of *Ghost Rider*, thoughts on Nintendo pricing, and commentary on the *Tenacious D* film.

## Timeline
- **The Golden Foundations**: Heavily active in the early 2000s, crafting the foundational lore Flashes of StrawberryClock.
- **The Critic Era**: Evolved into a cynical, yet protective, elder statesman who critiqued the increasing reliance on sprite comics and 5-second spam collabs.
