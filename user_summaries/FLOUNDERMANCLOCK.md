# FloundermanClock — Profile Summary

> Based on forum posts, 20 sampled threads, and a massive Newgrounds presence (669 fans, 2,045 saves, and 20+ movies).

FloundermanClock is undeniably a cornerstone of the Clock Crew’s creative output and high-level forum culture. An artist, animator, and eventual super moderator, Flounderman's presence is marked by a mix of cynical self-deprecation, absurdist anime humor, and a genuine, protective love for the Crew's creative standard.

## Identity
- **Aliases**: FloundermanClock. Frequently jokes about being famous internet animators to newcomers: *"Welcome to the Clock Crew, my name is FloundermanClock but you probably know me better as Legendary Frog"* or *"You might know me on newgrounds as Egoraptor."*
- **Roleplay/Persona**: Embraces a highly sarcastic, shape-shifting persona, occasionally referring to himself as "the shape shifting master of anime."

## Artistic Talent & Output
Unlike many members who just posted in the forum, FloundermanClock is a highly active, celebrated Newgrounds animator with over 20 movies and nearly 700 fans.
- **Movies**: His catalog reveals a mix of inside jokes, high-concept absurdity, and actual animation skill. Titles range from genuine-sounding pieces like "Are You Ready to Rock? (3.86)" and "High Literature (4.21)" to bizarre CC shitposts like "Space Adventure Flounderman -GAIDEN-", "Flounderman's Lunchquest (4.16)", and "8X_CLOCKDAYCARPETBOMBING.ZIP".
- **Mentorship/Critique**: Flounderman took the animation side of the crew seriously, acting as both an inspiration to junior members (Flan Clock calls him "one of my inspirations") and a gatekeeper against low-effort content: 
  > *"I recommend you not make sprite movies unless they are sprites made by yourself. Generally the clock crew likes to poke fun at the crowd that makes movies out of sonic the hedgehog sprites..."*

## Personality & Humor
Flounderman’s humor is dark, sarcastic, and unapologetically nerdy.
- **Anime and Oddities**: Obsessed with absurd anime references ("Boku no Pico", "Gundam GQuuuuuuX", "dagobah_lolicatgirls_anime.swf") and bizarre thought experiments ("I am totally starting the toupee troup. But instead of toupees for faces we just have blank fruits and veggies with toupees.").
- **Cranky but Caring**: Regularly insults people with affection. He frequently references getting "VERY MAD" or unleashing "UNSPEAKABLE EVIL," but actively welcomes new members who "arent morons so we're nice to the good ones." 

## Community Role
- **Super Moderator (and Rogue Admin)**: Flounderman wielded actual administrative power. As a mod, he controlled the "flood gates." He jokes about his mod status dramatically: *"Before the final blow was struck, I tore open a portal in time... I, flounderman, the shape shifting master of anime, unleashed an UNSPEAKABLE EVIL. You're welcome clock crew."*
- **The Vanguard of "Clock Quality"**: Flounderman was defensive about what the Clock Crew produced versus the rest of Newgrounds. He openly criticized ex-members (like RupeeClock) who leaned into generic NG trends: 
  > *"Rupee's movies have little to do with traditional clock crew humor, they generally appeal to the lowest common denominator of newgrounds."*

## Interests & Tastes
- **Memes & Internet Culture**: Openly critiques content theft ("gif sites are terrible and are basically ebaumsworld where they just steal content"), dissects "furry culture," and jokes about everything from Homestuck fat-fetish art to obscure NG lore.
- **Nihilism / Absurdity**: His threads ("Worst movies you have ever seen", "Jesus christ", "Greensburg Man Charged With Raping baby") range between cultural critique, sheer shock value, and deep web navel-gazing.

## Timeline
- **Golden Era Participant**: Built his reputation actively mocking 2005-era sprite flash while establishing himself as a premium Clock animator.
- **The Mod Era**: Reached super-moderator status, actively unbanning members to unleash chaos, and leading the creative charge on subsequent Clock Days.
- **Modern Era**: Remained active far later than most, contributing to massive zip-file collabs and "carpet bombing" releases on Clock Day while continuing to mentor new artists.
