# Slurpee — Profile Summary

> Based on 12,994 forum posts, 20 sampled threads, and a linked Newgrounds footprint.

Slurpee (also known as SlurpeeClock) was an incredibly prolific, long-standing pillar of the Clock Crew forums, maintaining an active and vocal presence over a 15-year span. With nearly 13,000 posts, Slurpee was embedded deep in the social fabric of the community, serving as both a historian for early-aughts forum drama and an opinionated regular who wasn't afraid to call out bad behavior.

## Identity
- **Aliases**: Slurpee, SlurpeeClock. Mentions formerly being "geeyouknit" in "wtfnoob" (an old community/forum group).
- **Associated Crews**: Mentions being around since the early days and tracks the history of rival factions like the F-Krew ("some completely unrelated bullshit that didn't show up until way later").
- **Vibe**: Direct, nostalgic, highly engaged with the intricacies of community lore, and politically/socially opinionated. 

## Personality
Slurpee exhibits a sharp, zero-tolerance attitude toward poor forum etiquette, while simultaneously being a welcoming figure to returning nostalgic members ("wa-hey!", "hello!"). 

His humor is dry and internet-native ("lol u guys are so nerd"), but he visibly drops the irony when actual ethical issues arise. He has a notable argumentative streak, particularly when debating the social implications of edgy early-2000s humor. He famously clashed with users like "Lunchbox Clock" and "DryIce" over racist jokes, refusing to accept the "we're just being offensive for fun" defense:
> *"Drop the subject. No more racism. Period... Racist jokes are only funny when they make fun of racism, and not race."*

When challenged as being an "uptight, pro-affirmative action left-wing-hippy-douchebag," Slurpee sharply responded:
> *"Racist jokes stir up shit that we don't fucking need to be stirred up; both here and in real life. Just bite your fucking tongue before you kick up a shit storm; is it really that hard?"*

## Community Role
- **Historian & Elder**: Slurpee consistently contextualizes the community's past for returning members. He recalls old collabs (like "the runs," organized by leekclock in 2005) and documents the lineage of community site designs (referencing classic NG designers like Stamper, tritoch, rccolaclock, and victrolaface).
- **The Welcoming Committee**: Heavily active in greeting returning alumni. He even pitched the idea of an "Alumni section" so old Clocks could post their "mini bios and where they are now" after a ten-year gap.
- **Debater / Voice of Reason**: Did not hesitate to aggressively shut down younger/edgier users ("you damn fool", "I cannot sanction this buffoonery").

## Interests & Tastes
- **Film & TV**: Heavy enthusiast for alternative comedy and film. Started threads celebrating *Mr. Show*, *The Cube (1969)*, and Edgar Wright's *Scott Pilgrim vs. The World* ("Michael Cera cast for title role in Scott Pilgrim. Edgar. Fucking. Wright. Directing.").
- **Gaming**: Casual mentions of games like *Portal* and *Metal Gear Solid*.
- **Politics**: Displays progressive, anti-bigotry stances, actively combating the casual racism prevalent in early internet circles.

## Timeline
- **Pre-2005**: Implied involvement with earlier internet groups like "wtfnoob".
- **2005 - 2010 (The Golden Era)**: Extremely active, hitting his stride in cultural debates, movie recommendations, and massive post-count generation.
- **2015**: Attempted to drum up hype with older members ("wE ARE BUILDING OUR BIG COMEBACK TOUR").
- **2017 - 2020**: Shifted into elder-statesman mode, popping in to reminisce ("clock crew memory lane sponsored by pepperidge farm"), critique returning members, and recommend "Pass The Ca$h" as essential viewing for latecomers catching up on the CC renaissance.
