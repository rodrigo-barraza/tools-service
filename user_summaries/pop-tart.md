# pop-tart — Profile Summary

> Based on roughly 8,702 forum posts, various sampled threads, and his signature sharp commentary spanning an incredible 9 years of activity.

pop-tart was a highly vocal, long-enduring, and remarkably sharp European regular within the Clock Crew forums. Operating with the custom title "A nice guy with an axe," he was as likely to help someone with technical advice as he was to brutally mock them for poor grammar or thin skin.

## Identity & Timeline
- **Alias**: pop-tart
- **Location**: Based in Norway ("We just got taco bell here in norway 1 year ago.") He also joked his location was "Norway, and in space/hell".
- **Years Active**: Registered on January 08, 2006, maintaining a steady, highly logged presence until roughly June 21, 2015.
- **Post Count**: 8,702 posts.
- **Custom Title**: "A nice guy with an axe."

## Personality & Humor
- **Grammar & Effort Police**: As a non-native English speaker himself (presumably, given his location), he had absolutely zero patience for members who refused to construct coherent sentences:
  > *"Do you even know English well or do you guess your way over the keyboard?"*
- **Abrasive but Helpful**: He matched the forum's heavy sarcasm tone perfectly. He could be incredibly hostile, but was known to drop the act completely if someone had a genuine site or tech issue.
- **Anti-Spam / Anti-Drama**: He possessed a low tolerance for attention-seeking behaviors or the manufactured drama that often populated the boards. He was a frequent commentator on the forum's structural meta ("the Troll Box," rules around the spam forum).

## Community Role
- **The Grounded Cynic**: While others indulged in massive meta-narratives or roleplay, pop-tart served as the reality check. He cut through hype and complained when site features broke or degraded:
  > *"What really pisses me off is they removed favorites from authors..."*
- **Longevity**: Surviving nearly a decade on the board meant pop-tart became part of the establishment, seeing multiple generations of "newbies" come, flame out, and vanish.

## Interests
- **Platform Infrastructure**: Frequently critiqued Newgrounds' layout changes, updates, and moderation meta.
- **Global Culture**: Occasionally brought European/Norwegian perspectives to heavily American-dominated threads, famously noting the delayed arrival of American fast food to his country.
