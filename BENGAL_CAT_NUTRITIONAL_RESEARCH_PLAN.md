# Bengal Cat Comprehensive Nutritional Requirements Research Plan

## Objective
To aggregate, normalize, and document the precise nutritional requirements (micro and macronutrients) for the **Bengal Cat** (Asian Leopard Cat x Domestic Cat hybrid), strictly integrating global veterinary and pet food industry standards while accounting for the breed's unique high-metabolism and physiological leanings.

## 1. Demographic Baseline & Breed Variations
- **Target Audience:** Healthy adult Bengal Cat.
- **Life Stage:** Adult Maintenance (excluding kittens/growth, pregnancy, and lactation).
- **Breed-Specific Nuance:** 
  - **High Metabolism & Musculature:** Bengals require a higher daily caloric intake for maintenance. While standard cats scale with $100 \times BW^{0.67}$, active Bengals often require energy factors of $1.2$ to $1.4$ against basal metabolic rates.
  - **Protein Bias:** Bengal breeders and veterinary nutritionists strongly recommend higher baseline protein ratios than AAFCO minimums to support their lean muscle mass and prevent wasting.
  - **Taurine Priority:** Due to a breed predisposition to hypertrophic cardiomyopathy (HCM), bioavailable taurine (often sourced from raw or wet muscle meats) is highly prioritized above minimum levels.
  - **Carbohydrate Intolerance:** Like many early-generation hybrids, Bengals frequently present with sensitive digestion and intolerance to high dietary carbohydrates, reinforcing a high-meat/low-filler nutritional split.

## 2. Targeted Global Standards

This research extracts baseline figures from three primary global guidelines:
1. **NRC (National Research Council) - 2006** (Recommended Allowance - RA)
2. **AAFCO (US Standard)** (Minimums and Maximums)
3. **FEDIAF (EU Standard)** (Minimum Recommendations and Nutritional Maximums)

*(Note: There is no legally distinct AAFCO/FEDIAF category for "Bengal Cats." Food is labeled for "Feline Maintenance." The tables below reflect these strict feline minimums, but notes emphasize Bengal-specific optimal targets.)*
